About Me

I am a postdoc with Nathan Klein at Boston University. Previously, I was a postdoc with Daniel Dadush at CWI Amsterdam. I completed my PhD in mathematics at the London School of Economics, under the supervision of László Végh. Before that, I studied mathematics at the University of Waterloo, where I was advised by Laura Sanità.

My research interests are in algorithms, optimization and game theory.

Publications

On Circuit Diameter and Straight Line Complexity

with Daniel Dadush and Stefan Kober
Conference version: IPCO 2026 (To appear)

  • arXiv
We show that the circuit diameter of a polyhedron can be bounded in terms of the straight line complexity of its associated LPs. We also give a circuit augmentation algorithm with matching iteration complexity.

Approximating the Held–Karp Bound for Metric TSP in Nearly Linear Work and Polylogarithmic Depth

with Omri Weinstein and Sorrachai Yingchareonthawornchai
Conference version: STOC 2025

  • arXiv
  • Slides
We give a nearly linear work parallel approximation scheme for the Held–Karp bound for metric TSP. Our method also yields a parallel approximation scheme for the k-edge-connected spanning subgraph LP, with similar work and depth bounds.

Online Matching on 3-Uniform Hypergraphs

with Sander Borst and Danish Kashaev
Conference version: IPCO 2025

  • arXiv
We study the online matching problem on k-uniform hypergraphs with one-sided vertex arrivals. The greedy algorithm is 1/k-competitive, whereas no algorithm is 2/k-competitive. For k = 3, we give a fractional algorithm which is (e − 1)/(e + 1)-competitive, and prove that this is optimal. For k ≥ 3, we give a simple integral algorithm which performs better than greedy when the online nodes have bounded degree.

A Strongly Polynomial Algorithm for Linear Programs with At Most Two Nonzero Entries per Row or Column

with Daniel Dadush, Bento Natura, Neil Olver and László A. Végh
Conference version: STOC 2024

  • Slides
  • Video
We give a strongly polynomial algorithm for minimum cost generalized flow, and as a consequence, for all LPs with at most two nonzero entries per row or column.

On the Correlation Gap of Matroids

with Edin Husić, Georg Loho and László A. Végh
Mathematical Programming, 2025.
Conference version: IPCO 2023

  • arXiv
  • Slides
We derive an improved bound on the correlation gap of matroid rank functions, parameterized by the rank and girth of the matroid. We also show that for any matroid, the correlation gap of its weighted matroid rank function is the worst under uniform weights.

Beyond Value Iteration for Parity Games: Strategy Iteration with Universal Trees

with Georg Loho
Logical Methods in Computer Science, 2025.
Conference version: MFCS 2022

  • arXiv
  • Slides
We propose a strategy iteration framework for parity games, which pivots based on the valuation given by any universal tree.

On Circuit Diameter Bounds via Circuit Imbalances

with Daniel Dadush, Bento Natura and László A. Végh
Mathematical Programming, 2024.
Conference version: IPCO 2022

  • arXiv
  • Slides
We show that the circuit diameter of a polyhedron can be bounded in terms of the circuit imbalance measure of its constraint matrix. We also give a circuit augmentation algorithm with similar iteration complexity.

An Accelerated Newton–Dinkelbach Method and its Application to Two Variables Per Inequality Systems

with Daniel Dadush, Bento Natura and László A. Végh
Mathematics of Operations Research, 2023.
Conference version: ESA 2021

  • arXiv
  • Slides
We give an accelerated, or 'look-ahead' version of the Newton–Dinkelbach method, and demonstrate its efficacy in 3 application domains: (i) An improved convergence bound for linear fractional combinatorial optimization; (ii) A strongly polynomial label-correcting algorithm for solving the feasibility of linear systems with at most two variables per inequality; (iii) A simplified variant of the parametric submodular function minimization result by Goemans et al. (2017).

An Efficient Characterization of Submodular Spanning Tree Games

with Laura Sanità
Mathematical Programming, 2020.
Conference version: IPCO 2019

  • arXiv
  • Slides
We study the minimum-cost spanning tree game, a classic model in cooperative game theory. We show how to check whether an instance of the game is convex/submodular in polynomial time.

Stabilizing Weighted Graphs

with Laura Sanità
Mathematics of Operations Research, 2020.
Conference version: ICALP 2018

  • arXiv
  • Slides
  • Video
An edge-weighted graph G is stable if the value of a maximum-weight matching equals the value of a maximum-weight fractional matching. We give a polynomial-time algorithm for finding a smallest set of vertices whose removal from G yields a stable graph. In contrast, we show that the problem of finding a smallest set of edges whose removal from G yields a stable graph does not admit any constant-factor approximation, unless P = NP.
